# IPL 2025 Team-wise Best XI

Built from the uploaded **2025 batting** and **2025 bowling** tables. Batting stats are shown as `runs, average, strike rate`; bowling stats are shown as `wickets, average, economy`.

## Selection approach

- Picked a role-balanced XI for each team: top order, middle order, wicketkeeper/finisher, all-rounders, spinners, and pacers.
- Batting-heavy players were judged mainly by runs, average, strike rate, and team-run share.
- Bowlers were judged mainly by wickets, average, economy, and whether the XI already had enough pace/spin balance.
- Where two players were close, I preferred the player who improved team balance.

## Tough calls / notes

- **CSK:** I kept Ayush Mhatre and Dewald Brevis despite fewer matches because their strike rates were much stronger than the slower alternatives. Noor Ahmad was the obvious lead bowler with 24 wickets.
- **DC:** Mustafizur Rahman gets the last pace slot despite only 3 matches because the bowling quality was stronger than the lower-impact options.
- **GT:** The top three almost pick themselves: Sai Sudharsan, Shubman Gill, and Jos Buttler. Rashid Khan’s batting was limited this season, but he still balances the XI as a spinner/all-rounder.
- **KKR:** Quinton de Kock is included for wicketkeeping balance, while Narine, Russell, Moeen, Varun, Vaibhav Arora, and Harshit Rana give six bowling options.
- **LSG:** Will O’Rourke is included as the extra strike pacer despite fewer games because the attack otherwise becomes too spin/all-rounder dependent.
- **MI:** Karn Sharma and Vignesh Puthur had good wicket returns, but Santner gives better batting depth and control; Bumrah and Boult remain the main strike pair.
- **PBKS:** Punjab had the cleanest balance: strong top six plus Stoinis/Jansen, two spinners, and Arshdeep.
- **RR:** Vaibhav Sooryavanshi’s 206.56 strike rate makes him worth the opener slot even from 7 matches. Hasaranga, Archer, Theekshana, and Sandeep form the main attack.
- **RCB:** Romario Shepherd was close, but the XI already had enough hitting; Krunal, Hazlewood, Bhuvneshwar, and Dayal give better bowling structure.
- **SRH:** Unadkat and Eshan Malinga are picked ahead of some higher-profile names because their wicket impact and economy were better in this table.
