## Notes on tough calls

- **CSK:** Harbhajan Singh stays ahead of Shardul Thakur because he took 16 wickets at 7.09 economy, giving CSK a stronger spin core with Tahir and Jadeja.
- **DC:** Sandeep Lamichhane gets the second wrist-spin slot because Delhi already has enough pace through Rabada, Ishant, and Morris.
- **KKR:** Bowling was the weak point. Gurney and Prasidh Krishna are included to keep the XI structurally valid, even though Narine, Chawla, and Russell carried most of the wicket-taking.
- **MI:** Behrendorff is picked as the third specialist pacer; McClenaghan had fewer wickets in this season, while Bumrah/Malinga/Hardik/Krunal/Rahul Chahar cover the core attack.
- **Punjab Kings:** Nicholas Pooran is included for finishing impact, while Sam Curran gives the XI a left-arm seam all-rounder.
- **RR:** Oshane Thomas edges Ish Sodhi because the XI already has Shreyas Gopal as the main spinner and needed an extra pace option.
- **RCB:** Dale Steyn is included despite limited matches because his 4 wickets at 17.25 gave RCB better quality than most other pace options.
- **SRH:** Sandeep Sharma and Siddarth Kaul support Bhuvneshwar and Khaleel, while Nabi/Rashid give the spin all-rounder balance.
